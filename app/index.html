Instructions

Begin with Google Maps. Search for any locations that comes to your mind.

Find out about the direction that Maps offers to you.

Look for sharing the selected walking directions.

Tap on ⋮ to find the Share directions.

Among the offered apps find and tap Copy to clipboar.

Open adrift.digital in your browser and tap on ＋ to add a new drift.

Paste the shared directions from the clipboard (you can also tap on the paste button to the buttom right)
If you haven't done the previous steps, you can paste a sample directions text by tapping on the button to the buttom left.

After pasting the directions text, tap on the Get Lost.

Btw, you can also type a simple Maps direction text manually :) The image following shows a simple acceptable syntax.

After previewing the new directions tap on the Start Drift to start drifting in the city.

Follow the directions, answer the questions at each step in form of conversation with your friends about issue and the surrounding. Have fun and follow your intuitions :)
Record your conversations by tapping on the Record button (circle).

Stop the recording session by tapping on the Stop button (square).

Before tapping the Next button in order to proceed, you can download your recording by taping on the download button. This is helpful if you desire to perform another recording, as the page saves only the latest recording.

Tap on Finish button at the final step. The directions and recording stuff are done, yet continue to your drift, discovery and imagination.

On the preview page you can download all the recording you've done so far.

Finally, on the first page, you can see the drifts performed both compelted and uncompleted ones. Tap on any of them to preview the completed steps, download the recordings and delete the drifts.

About the project

adrift

Mapping repressed topographies

By keeping us in [digital] space, mobile devices and technologies like Google Maps hinder the potential of constructing meaning in the city. Using the same infrastructure, Adrift app constructs situations by deconstructing the imposed directional text to bring back the possibility of getting lost in the urban fabric. By doing so, it aims to map the disparate, repressed or unavailable topographies and revive the [missing] spatial experiments of everyday life.

Development and the app

Adrift is a front-end single page application (SPA) written in vanilla Javascript.

The applications data storage is currently sessionStorage, which means after the page is reload the data will be cleaned. Another method for saving data including media files (voice recordings (blobs) here) is to use cloud services, yet regarding data privacy issues and concerns, it is prefered to use client-side storage (permenatly on the device storage by downloading and temporarily on the browser with Web Storage). Hence, to save the voice recordings on the devices by tapping/clicking on specifed download buttons, is to save the drifts.

Unlike sessionStorage, localStorage can rather keep simple data (here directions, steps, orders) for later visit of the page whick can be implemented in later versions.

Adrift uses modern web API such as MediaStream Recording API and Clipboard API, which currently are not supported by iOS devices.
